# Target Customer Segment Analysis
#### Project Background
Founded in 2002, Quantium has a long history of innovation in data science across all sectors of the economy. As a part of Quantium’s Retail Analytics team, we have been approached by a leading snack-food retailer who wants to better understand customer purchasing behaviour and identify high-value customer segments to support targeted marketing and product placement strategies. The business had accumulated extensive transaction-level sales data, customer demographic information, and product details, which had not been fully leveraged for strategic decision-making.

This project analyzes customer purchasing patterns across different life stages, spending segments, brands, and pack sizes to uncover the key drivers of sales and customer engagement.

Insights and recommendations are provided on the following key areas:

**Customer Segment Analysis**: Evaluation of customer segments based on life stage and spending behaviour to identify the groups contributing the highest sales, customer counts, transaction volumes, and product purchases.

**Purchasing Behaviour Analysis**: Assessment of transaction frequency, units purchased per customer, and spending patterns to understand how purchasing habits vary across customer segments.

**Product and Brand Performance**: Analysis of brand preferences and product performance to identify the brands driving the highest sales and customer demand across different customer groups.

**Pack Size Analysis**: Investigation of pack size preferences and their impact on sales performance to determine which product sizes contribute most significantly to revenue generation.

**Pricing Analysis**: Evaluation of average price per unit across customer segments, brands, and pack sizes to understand customer willingness to pay and identify potential premium purchasing behaviour.

The objective of this analysis is to identify the most valuable customer segments, understand the factors driving their purchasing decisions, and provide data-driven recommendations for targeted marketing, product assortment, and growth opportunities.

#### Data Structure & Initial Checks
The analysis was conducted using retail transaction and customer demographic datasets containing information on purchases, customer segments, product details, and sales performance.


Prior to analysis, data quality checks and preprocessing were performed, including handling missing values, identifying outliers, extracting brands and pack sizes from product names, and validating customer segment classifications.
Python (Pandas) was used for data cleaning and exploratory analysis, while Tableau was used to develop interactive dashboards and visualize customer purchasing behaviour.

#### Executive Summary

The analysis identified Budget Older Families, Mainstream Young Singles/Couples, and Retirees as the most valuable customer segments. Budget Older Families generated the highest sales, units sold, and transaction frequency, while Mainstream Young Singles/Couples represented the largest customer base.
Kettle, Doritos, Smiths, and Pringles emerged as the leading brands driving sales, with 175g packs being the most popular pack size across customers. These findings suggest that marketing and promotional efforts should focus on high-value customer segments while prioritizing top-performing brands and pack sizes.

#### Deep Dive into Insights

**1. Customer Segment Performance**
Budget Older Families emerged as the highest-value customer segment, generating the highest sales, purchasing the most units, and exhibiting the highest transaction frequency. Mainstream Young Singles/Couples represented the largest customer base, while Retirees also contributed significantly to overall revenue.

**2. Purchasing Behaviour**
Older Families and Young Families recorded the highest number of units purchased per customer and the highest transaction frequency, indicating stronger repeat purchase behaviour than other segments.

**3. Brand Preferences**
Kettle, Doritos, Smiths, and Pringles were the strongest-performing brands, with Kettle consistently driving the highest sales across multiple customer segments.

**4. Pack Size Preferences**
The 175g pack size was the most popular across customer segments and contributed substantially to overall sales. Larger pack sizes generally generated higher revenue than smaller packs.

**5. Pricing Insights**
Average price per unit showed minimal variation across customer segments, suggesting that differences in sales performance are driven primarily by purchasing frequency and volume rather than price sensitivity.

#### Recommendations

Based on the analysis, the following recommendations are proposed to improve sales performance and customer engagement:

**1. Focus Marketing Efforts on High-Value Customer Segments**
Budget Older Families, Mainstream Young Singles/Couples, and Retirees were identified as the most valuable customer groups based on their contribution to sales, customer volume, and purchasing frequency. Targeted marketing campaigns should prioritize these segments to maximize return on investment.

**2. Leverage High-Performing Brands**
Kettle, Doritos, Smiths, and Pringles consistently generated strong sales across customer segments. Promotional campaigns, product placements, and cross-selling strategies should emphasize these brands to drive additional revenue.

**3. Prioritize Popular Pack Sizes**
The 175g pack size emerged as the most preferred across customer segments and contributed significantly to overall sales. Ensuring consistent availability and incorporating these pack sizes into promotional offers can help maintain strong sales performance.

**4. Encourage Repeat Purchases**
Older Families and Young Families demonstrated the highest transaction frequency and units purchased per customer. Loyalty rewards, bundle offers, and multi-buy promotions can be used to further strengthen repeat purchasing behaviour.

**5. Optimize Product Assortment**
Since sales performance appears to be driven more by purchasing frequency and product preference than by price differences, inventory and merchandising decisions should focus on high-demand brands and pack sizes rather than aggressive price reductions.
